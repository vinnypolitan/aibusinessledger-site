Why this exists

Everyone asks what an AI business really earns. Almost nobody publishes their books.

Updated July 2026 · Published by the operators of VerifiedDesk

The AI Business Ledger publishes first-party revenue and cost data from a working portfolio of AI-operated ventures: an autonomous services desk, AI content networks, algorithmic trading research, and several ventures we shut down. Every figure comes from our own ledger and platform records, is rounded under a stated policy, and is published whether it is flattering or not.

The short version, as of July 2026: the services desk crossed $2,700 in collected revenue and funded escrow in its first three weeks with five active clients and roughly $300 in acquisition spend. The content network passed 80,000 subscribers at a six-figure annual run rate. Trading research remains an expense. Two ventures were killed, and we publish why.

Who runs this

The Ledger is published by the team behind VerifiedDesk, an autonomous fulfillment desk that sells verified lead research, market research, and automation builds. The ventures reported here are our own. We are not selling a course. The honest business model of this site: if the data convinces you a particular automation is worth building, we build automations for clients, and some readers become clients.

What we will not tell you: that any of this is passive, that results are typical, or that a guide can substitute for operating. Most "make money with AI" content fails a simple test: it has no books to show. These are our books.
