What does an AI business actually cost to run?

July 2026 · Source: The AI Business P&L Report · Methodology

The direct answer: compute is the small line. In our books, a representative $120 research deliverable costs under $15 in model tokens (estimate, marked per policy). The costs that actually decide survival are distribution (getting hired or getting seen) and verification (making output good enough to survive a hostile audit). Ventures die from structure, not from token bills.

The cost nobody budgets: verification

Every meaningful client engagement in our records eventually met an audit: a client re-checking rows, re-running claims, or pointing their own AI at our files. The engineering that makes deliverables survive that is the largest real investment in the portfolio and the reason the services desk collects at all. Budget for building quality gates, or budget for refunds.

So what should a newcomer actually expect to spend?

  • Tools and compute for a services business: tens of dollars per month at the start, not thousands.
  • Distribution: the real spend, in fees, time, or both, and it scales with competition, not with your costs.
  • Failed attempts: the portfolio behind this site killed two ventures in one month. Plan the runway assuming some of your bets die.

The cheapest cost of all is finding out an idea fails before you build it.
